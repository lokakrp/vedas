Dictionary

List of Sanskrit words within the Ṛgveda

Concepts

  • Brahman: Ultimate reality or universal principle
  • Ṛta: Cosmic order or truth maintained through rituals and sacrifices
  • Dharma: Moral duty, righteousness, or cosmic law
  • Ātman: Self or soul
  • Karma: Action, deed, or the principle of cause and effect
  • Satya: Truth
  • Tapas: Austerity, spiritual practice
  • Deva: Deity or god
  • Asura: Demon or anti-god
  • Māyā: Illusion or magic
  • Rāṣṭra: Kingdom or realm
  • Ṛṣi: Sage or seer
  • Mantra: Sacred utterance or sound
  • Vāc: Speech
  • Sāma: Melody, song, or chant
  • Loka: World or realm
  • Svarga: Heaven or celestial realm
  • Naraka: Hell or underworld

Vedic Rituals

  • Yajña: Ritual sacrifice or offering
  • Agnihotra: Daily fire ritual
  • Homa: Fire ritual involving offerings
  • Upākarma: Annual ritual of changing the sacred thread
  • Prāyaścitta: Atonement or expiatory ritual
  • Śrāddha: Ancestor worship ritual
  • Avabhṛtha: Post-ritual bath
  • Samidh: Ritual kindling of fire
  • Abhiṣeka: Ritual anointment
  • Homāgni: The fire used in sacrificial rituals
  • Ājya: Ghee or clarified butter used in rituals
  • Yāga: Ritual sacrifice or worship
  • Āhuti: Oblation, offering into the fire
  • Śānti: Ritual for peace or tranquility
  • Puroḍāśa: Ritual cake or offering
  • Prakṣālaṇa: Ritual ablution or cleansing
  • Sūrya Namaskāra: Sun salutation ritual

Family

  • Gṛha: Home or household
  • Ātman: Soul or self
  • Janman: Birth or lineage
  • Pitṛ: Father, ancestor
  • Mātṛ: Mother
  • Putrā: Son
  • Duhitā: Daughter
  • Svāsara: Brother-in-law or sister-in-law
  • Bhāgina: Sister
  • Bhrātṛ: Brother
  • Śvaśura: Father-in-law
  • Śvashura: Mother-in-law
  • Samdhi: Father-in-law or mother-in-law (depending on context)
  • Snushā: Daughter-in-law
  • Daivā: Sister-in-law
  • Devā: Brother-in-law
  • Samdam: Parents-in-law
  • Prabhu: Master, lord, husband
  • Patni: Wife

Groups

  • Ārya: A member of Vedic society, who is noble and honorable
  • Dasyu: Enemy, demon, or robber (often used for people perceived as hostile or non-Vedic)
  • Varṇa: Social class or caste division
  • Dvija: Twice-born, referring to initiation ceremony
  • Jāti: Birth group or sub-caste
  • Janapada: Nation or country
  • Samāja: Community or assembly
  • Kula: Family or clan
  • Gotra: Lineage or clan
  • Viś: Settlement
  • Gana: Group, multitude, or tribe
  • Brāhmaṇa: Priest or scholarly class
  • Kṣatriya: Warrior or ruling class
  • Vaiśya: Merchant or agricultural class, a member of the viś
  • Śūdra: Servant or laborer class, typically non-ārya who became servants
  • Sākhā: Branch or school (of a Vedic tradition)
  • Vṛnda: Group or assembly
  • Dāsa: Servant, slave, or bondsman
  • Dāsī: Female servant or slave
  • Mitra: Friend or ally
  • Śatru: Enemy or adversary
  • Yajamāna: Sacrificer or patron (the person for whom the sacrifice is performed)
  • Purohita: Family priest or chief priest

Animals

  • Gau: Cow
  • Aśva: Horse
  • Ukṣā: Cow
  • Vṛṣabha: Bull
  • Varāha: Boar
  • Mriga: Deer
  • Haṁsa: Swan or goose
  • Kṛśānu: Antelope
  • Śarabha: Mythical creature, sometimes interpreted as a deer or antelope
  • Vṛka: Wolf
  • Ṛkṣa: Bear
  • Varaṇa: Boar or pig
  • Mṛgā: Doe or hind
  • Khara: Ass or donkey
  • Uṣṭra: Camel
  • Śaśa: Hare or rabbit
  • Śyena: Falcon or hawk
  • Garuda: Eagle, specifically the mythical bird associated with Vishnu
  • Mayūra: Peacock
  • Kaṇṭaka: Porcupine
  • Vāraha: Wild boar
  • Meshá: Ram or sheep

Food and Drink

  • Ājya: Ghee or clarified butter
  • Soma: Sacred ritual drink
  • Māṁsa: Meat
  • Aśva-māṁsa: Horse meat, specifically used in sacrificial rituals
  • Go-māṁsa: Cow meat
  • Matsya: Fish
  • Puroḍāśa: Oblation or offering made of grain or flour
  • Pāyasa: Rice pudding or milk porridge
  • Madhu: Honey
  • Apām: Water
  • Phāṇita: Sweetened rice or rice mixed with honey
  • Dadhi: Curd or yogurt
  • Piśāca: Possibly a fermented drink or a kind of food, mentioned in ritual contexts
  • Ana: Food or grain, generally offered in rituals
  • Śuddhi: Purified or consecrated food, prepared for rituals
  • Karambha: Food offering
  • Bārhaspatya: Soma mixed with milk and curds
  • Yavasa: Soma drink
  • Medhya: A kind of food used in rituals
  • Udaka: Water, especially used in rituals
  • Anna: Food, grain, or rice
  • Madya: Fermented or intoxicating drink
  • Prāṇa: A beverage or liquid substance, possibly related to soma
  • Piśitāśana: Cooked food or meal
  • Vājī: Beverage or drink, possibly related to soma
  • Kukkuṭa-māṁsa: Chicken meat
  • Śaśī-māṁsa: Hare or rabbit meat
  • Meṣa-māṁsa: Goat or sheep meat

Deities

Nature and Elements

  • Vāyu: Wind or air.
  • Āpa: Water.
  • Prithvī: Earth.
  • Ākāśa: Sky or space.

Time and Seasons

  • Ritu: Season.
  • Samvatsara: Year or cycle of time.
